# The Beginning

After graduating, I started thinking about personal projects I could work on while job hunting — something to keep my skills sharp and give me a creative outlet. I cycled through a few ideas before landing on a familiar thought:

*Why not make a small game?*

Of course, “small” quickly turned into pages of brainstorming, design notes, and scattered ideas — but not a single line of code yet. I haven’t even chosen a game engine. Still, that’s part of the process, and this blog will serve as a record of that journey, whether the project grows into a finished game or simply a valuable learning experience.

# The Concept

An **apothecary**, according to Merriam-Webster, is “one who prepares and sells drugs or compounds for medicinal purposes,” while an **understudy** is “one prepared to take another’s role or duties.”

The idea behind *Apothecary Understudy* is simple: you play as a student learning the trade under an experienced apothecary in a cozy fantasy village.

The game is envisioned as a small life-simulation experience where players:

- interact with their mentor and local villagers  
- venture into the fields to gather herbs and ingredients  
- craft medicines through a potion-making minigame  
- manage a small storefront and sell remedies to NPCs  

Think of it as a relaxed, bite-sized life sim with a calm, cozy atmosphere — inspired by games that focus on routine, discovery, and small moments rather than high stakes.

# The Vision

Before committing to a specific engine or technical direction, I want to fully explore the game’s identity and core gameplay loop.

Currently, I imagine the game shifting perspectives depending on the player’s activity:

- **Town & shop scenes:** 2D presentation similar to a visual novel, focused on dialogue and character interaction.  
- **Field exploration:** a 3D isometric environment inspired by tactical RPG maps, allowing players to gather ingredients and explore.

This contrast between calm storytelling spaces and exploratory gathering areas is something I’m excited to experiment with.

I’ve already sketched several versions of the gameplay loop, which I’ll break down in the next post.
