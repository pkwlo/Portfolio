# The Beginning
After graduating from school, I was thinking of various personal projects to do to keep my skills up while looking for a job and after juggling a few different ideas, I decided why not try to make a simple game? Yeah simple, somehow as usual, I got way too into the planning and my design docs have way too much brainstorming and ideas and not a single line of code to start. In fact I haven't even decided the game engine to use yet. But that will come with time, for now I will continue to document my journey, whether or not it comes to fruition.

# The Concept
As per the Merriam Webster dictionary, an apothecary is "one who prepares and sells drugs or compounds for medicinal purposes" and an understudy is "one who is prepared to act another's part or take over another's duties". 

I wanted to make a short game where the concept was basically where you as the MC was studying under an apothecary to learn their trade and the genre is kind of like a little life sim where you interact with your teacher, go out into the field and gather herbs, have a medicine making minigame, and a little storefront minigame section where you sell your concotions to local NPCs in the village. I guess like a super simple version of Stardew Valley but that sort of chill vibe. 

# The Vision
Before choosing a game engine to work with I really want to flesh out the type of game and general gameplay loop first. 

So far, I imagine the game to be 2d similar to a visual novel when in town, in the store etc, whenever you're not in the fields. When you go out into the field, the perspective switches to a sort of 3d isometric map similar to the maps in the Disgaea series (which is one of my favourites!).

As for the gameplay loop, I've had a couple of renditions already which I will describe in a following post.
